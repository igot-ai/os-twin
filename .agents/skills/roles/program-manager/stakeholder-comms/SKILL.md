# stakeholder-comms

## Purpose

The best program execution is invisible to stakeholders — they only notice when things go wrong. This skill ensures stakeholders receive the right information at the right level of detail to make timely decisions.

## Communication Types

### 1. Weekly Status Update
**Audience:** All stakeholders
**Format:** Brief, scannable, action-oriented

```markdown
# Program Status — [Program Name] — Week of [Date]

**Overall:** 🟢 On Track | 🟡 At Risk | 🔴 Behind

## Headlines
- ✅ [Major accomplishment this week]
- ⚠️ [Key risk or issue]
- 📅 [Upcoming milestone]

## Milestone Summary
| Milestone | Due | Status |
|-----------|-----|--------|
| [name] | [date] | 🟢🟡🔴 |

## Decisions Needed
| Decision | By whom | By when | Impact of delay |
|----------|---------|---------|----------------|
| [decision] | [person] | [date] | [what happens if delayed] |

## Risks
| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|-----------|
| [risk] | H/M/L | H/M/L | [plan] |
```

### 2. Executive Summary
**Audience:** VP/C-level
**Rule:** < 5 bullet points, no jargon, decisions-first

```markdown
## [Program Name] — Executive Update

1. **Status:** [one sentence overall health]
2. **Key win:** [most impactful progress]
3. **Top risk:** [biggest threat to timeline/quality]
4. **Decision needed:** [what you need from them, by when]
5. **Next milestone:** [what ships next and when]
```

### 3. Escalation Request
**Audience:** Decision maker for the specific issue

```markdown
## Escalation: [Issue Title]

**Urgency:** [why this needs attention now]
**Impact:** [what happens if unresolved]
**Options:**
1. [Option A — trade-offs]
2. [Option B — trade-offs]
**Recommendation:** [what you suggest]
**Decision needed by:** [date]
```

## Communication Principles

1. **Lead with the ask** — if you need a decision, say so first
2. **No surprises** — escalate early, not when it's too late
3. **Right level of detail** — executives get headlines, teams get details
4. **Quantify impact** — "2 days delayed" is better than "slightly behind"
5. **Action-oriented** — every communication should drive an action

## Anti-Patterns

- Status reports that are just data dumps → distill into headlines and actions
- Burying bad news at the bottom → lead with risks and decisions
- Over-communicating → respect people's time; weekly is enough unless there's an escalation
- Using jargon with non-technical stakeholders → translate technical issues into business impact
